My Backstory

I started programming when I was around 13 years old. When I started it was mainly free projects & passion projects to track my progress. My main language I not only started on, but stuck with for 5+ years is Java. I learned a lot from Java with general programming knowledge. Java taught me the fundamentals, variables, primitives, asynchronous programming, documentation, apis, and naming conventions. The Java projects I developed were used for production. They needed to be very optimized & have a good code base. These projects I developed & sold were made for people to be able to build off of them with ease & not need to read through tons of documentation to understand it.

My Takeaway

My main takeaway from all of the projects I created in my time with Java is that no project is ever finished. There's always something you can add, tweak, modify, optimize, and so much more. You also can't look at the code like it's black and white there's much more ways to look at it and always other alternatives to make it run more effectively. I think that's something that sets me aside from other developers. I strive for a more perfect & efficient way of writing code. I also love learning and do projects on the basis of learning new things.
